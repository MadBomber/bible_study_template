## Week 8 Discussion Guide

Use these questions with your group or for personal reflection at the end of the week.

### Opening (5 minutes)

- Think of a time you received unexpected generosity from someone you had wronged. What did that feel like?

### Observation (10 minutes)

1. How does Judah in chapter 44 differ from Judah in chapter 37? What changed him?
2. What does Joseph do before accusing his brothers — and what does he never do? What does that sequence tell us about how he handles forgiveness?

### Interpretation (15 minutes)

3. Joseph says "God sent me" three times in Genesis 45. How does this framing of providence relate to — without denying — the brothers' real guilt?
4. How does Judah's substitutionary offer in chapter 44 prepare us to understand the cross?

### Application (15 minutes)

5. Joseph's forgiveness was not a feeling but a theological act — rooted in his understanding of God's sovereignty. What would it mean to forgive someone in your life through that same theological lens?
6. "You meant it for evil; God meant it for good." Is there a chapter of your own story you have not yet been able to read that way? What makes it difficult?

### Prayer (5 minutes)

Close in prayer for any in the group who are carrying unforgiveness — asking God to give them the long theology of Joseph, and the grace to move toward those who have hurt them.
